Legend

The work is arranged as infrastructure.

The through-line across my work is not a single mission, project, or institution. It is a recurring systems problem: how do people meaningfully move through systems whose rules, language, and stakes are not immediately visible?

That question shapes how I approach NASA mission engagement, informal learning networks, public science storytelling, higher education pathway research, and leadership strategy. The work is public-facing, but it is rarely only public communication. It is the design of conditions that allow public meaning to happen.

01

Mission Engagement

Designing public entry points into scientific futures

I support public engagement around NASA astrophysics missions by helping technical ambition become meaningful public orientation. With Roman, the work is not simply explaining the telescope. It is helping audiences, facilitators, ambassadors, educators, and host sites understand why Roman matters before the mission is already culturally legible.

My work supports the engagement infrastructure around that question: webinar preparation, presenter coordination, stakeholder communication, facilitator-facing framing, host-site activation, and audience onboarding for launch-era engagement.

02

Interpretive Infrastructure

Moving from information density to conceptual navigation

My Webb-related work focuses on how audiences move through complex images, mission milestones, scientific claims, and visual experiences without getting lost. This includes advisory work on STORIMap narrative structure, points of interest, guided tour pacing, visual storytelling, and public-facing explanatory content.

In recent Webb work, especially around Centaurus A and black-hole-related science features, my role has been to help teams align scientific precision with conceptual clarity and emotional entry. Public understanding is not only about what people are told. It is about whether they can find a path through what they are seeing.

  • STORIMap interpretation
  • Visual cognition
  • Guided tour pacing
  • Science readability

03

Informal Learning Networks

Building durable learning infrastructure across distributed systems

NASA’s Universe of Learning and the Informal Learning Network are where my work most clearly becomes ecosystem strategy. The network connects museums, libraries, science centers, children’s museums, community organizations, schools, universities, and informal educators who adapt NASA astrophysics resources for local communities.

I use partner feedback, evaluation findings, adoption patterns, and reach data to identify what the network needs next, from clearer resource navigation to stronger mini-networks and multi-format materials. The operating question is not only “How far did this reach?” but “What made participation possible, sustainable, and locally meaningful?”

  • Network strategy
  • Partner support
  • Evaluation-to-strategy synthesis
  • Resource usability

04

Recognition Governance

Studying how systems decide what counts

My graduate research asks the same question in another system: once people have done the work, can they reliably move forward? At Harvard, my independent study examines why promising pathways across apprenticeship, workforce learning, transfer, credit for prior learning, and degree programs often weaken at scale.

The core insight is that learning is not the scarce resource. Reliable recognition is. I am developing a diagnostic framework that follows the recognition chain: Learning → Evidence → Validation → Translation → Recording → Application → Advancement.

This work connects directly to my larger professional direction: helping institutions move from pathways that promise opportunity to pathways that produce it reliably.

  • Credit mobility
  • Learning-to-credit reliability
  • Institutional obligation
  • Postsecondary value

Navigation method

The page underneath the map.

  1. Start with the learner’s path, not the institution’s chart.Institutions organize work by teams, products, missions, departments, or funding lines. People experience it as a pathway. I look for the route a person actually has to travel.
  2. Translate without flattening.Complex ideas deserve clarity, not dilution. I look for structures that preserve rigor while creating entry.
  3. Treat relationships as infrastructure.Large-scale work holds through trust, timing, shared language, partner capacity, and the invisible labor of coordination.
  4. Build tools people can actually use.A beautiful strategy that cannot be implemented is a decorative object. I build briefs, frameworks, reports, guides, maps, and meeting structures that help people carry work forward.
  5. Design for dignity under scale.I care about systems that grow without losing their humanity: systems that remain accessible, relational, and trustworthy as they reach more people.
