Inside the volume

The work is arranged as infrastructure.

01

Mission engagement

Making NASA moments participatory

Webb, Roman, Hubble, and NASA’s Universe of Learning become more than announcements when audiences have pathways into the science. My work sits in that passageway: shaping learning arcs, host-site support, public-facing framing, and engagement resources that help mission milestones become usable.

02

Network stewardship

Holding the field, not just the event

Across museums, libraries, science centers, educators, community organizations, and agency partners, the work becomes relational infrastructure. The emphasis is coordination, shared language, partner readiness, and the invisible choreography that lets national science education feel locally possible.

03

Audience strategy

Designing for sensemaking

I translate complexity into interpretive choices: what needs to be felt first, what needs to be clarified, what can wait, and what kind of scaffold lets different publics recognize themselves in discovery without flattening the science.

04

Artifacts and systems

Building the tools behind the room

Audience briefs, facilitator guidance, project notes, narrative maps, STORI-style interpretive layers, webinar structures, and portfolio reviews all become instruments. Some are polished public products. Many are backstage tools that help teams make better decisions.

Operating method

The page underneath the page.

  1. Listen for the public question beneath the technical claim.
  2. Translate the science without sanding off its wonder or its difficulty.
  3. Build tools that help teams coordinate, not just communicate.
  4. Design for uptake: what people can do, host, explain, adapt, or carry forward.
