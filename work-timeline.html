Through-line

The work is arranged as infrastructure.

The through-line across my work is not a single mission, project, or institution. It is a recurring systems problem: how do people meaningfully move through systems whose rules, language, and stakes are not immediately visible?

I work where scientific accuracy, institutional priorities, audience cognition, partner capacity, cultural relevance, accessibility, evaluation, and trust all meet. The visible product may be a webinar, toolkit, story map, launch event, report, or public program. The deeper work is designing the conditions that allow public meaning to happen.

Operating method

How I work when the system gets complicated.

  1. Start with the learner’s path.Find the route a person actually has to travel, then design for the points where meaning, confidence, or access breaks down.
  2. Translate without flattening.Clarity is not dilution. The task is to preserve rigor while building enough structure for people to enter and keep moving.
  3. Treat relationships as infrastructure.Large-scale work travels through trust, timing, shared language, partner capacity, and the quiet labor of coordination.
  4. Build tools people can use.A beautiful strategy that cannot be implemented is a decorative object. Briefs, maps, guides, frameworks, and notes should help people make decisions.
  5. Design for dignity under scale.Scale should not become extraction, broadcast, or blur. The system has to remain legible, relational, and humane as it grows.
