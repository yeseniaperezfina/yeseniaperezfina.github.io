Leadership

How I Lead

My leadership style is calm, direct, and systems-minded. I make complex work more navigable without losing the texture of the people, institutions, and constraints inside it.

I treat every initiative as a learning system — for the audience and for the team running it.

The goal is not only to deliver, but to build structures that can keep learning as they move.

  • Strategic clarity
  • Cross-functional trust
  • Strong synthesis
  • Steady in ambiguity

What I protect

  • Clarity about purpose, roles, and constraints.
  • Design decisions grounded in audience reality, not assumption.
  • Enough structure for scale, enough flexibility for context.
  • Communication that makes coordination easier, not heavier.

How people experience me

  • Calm and clear under complexity.
  • Good at naming the pattern beneath the problem.
  • A bridge between scientists, educators, evaluators, and leadership.
  • Strong with language, strategy, and follow-through.

Digital practice

Digital + Design

I do not approach digital work only as a content stakeholder. I build, prototype, and refine experiences directly — using code, design systems, and interaction thinking to make ideas usable.

This site itself is part of that practice: concept, information architecture, interface design, copy, and front-end build.

What I build

  • Editorial-style portfolio sites and lightweight front-end experiences using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript.
  • Information architecture that helps readers move from narrative to proof quickly.
  • Interface systems that balance visual design, accessibility, performance, and legibility.

How that supports my leadership

  • I work effectively with designers and developers because I understand the constraints of the medium.
  • I prototype ideas directly, which sharpens product thinking and reduces ambiguity early.
  • I bring the same audience-centered lens to digital environments that I bring to museums, networks, and public programs.

Technical fluency

  • HTML, CSS, JavaScript, GitHub Pages, responsive layouts, interaction design, and design-system thinking.
  • Comfortable shaping front-end experiences independently and collaborating with technical teams on scope, flow, and usability.
  • Working style grounded in clarity, iteration, and lightweight, maintainable builds.

This site as a case study

  • Single-page information architecture designed for recruiter scanability and narrative coherence.
  • Custom visual system, interaction patterns, responsive behavior, reduced-motion support, and print styling.
  • Built and maintained without frameworks to keep the experience dependency-light, accessible, and transparent.
