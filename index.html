Practice

How I Work

Underneath every project is a way of working: how we ask questions, invite people in, and make decisions visible.

I treat every initiative as a learning ecosystem—a living structure of roles, rituals, constraints, and possibilities. The work is to make that structure legible, humane, and capable of holding real change.

That means noticing who has voice, who has margin, and how people actually move through the system—not just how it looks on a slide.

  • Systems lens
  • Pattern spotting
  • Sense-making under ambiguity
  • Care for people & pace

Designing the system, not just the moment

  • Start with the ecosystem: policies, people, incentives, and constraints.
  • Clarify roles, decision rights, and rhythms so teams know how work moves.
  • Design for sustainability—what will still work when pilot energy fades.

Making evidence usable

  • Co-create evaluation questions that matter to both funders and practitioners.
  • Translate data into patterns, tensions, and decisions—not just dashboards.
  • Close the loop by returning findings to the people whose work they describe.

Holding equity as a design constraint

  • Ask who benefits, who is burdened, and whose expertise is missing from the table.
  • Design roles, pathways, and communication so first-gen, transfer, and historically excluded communities can see themselves in the system.
  • Treat belonging as architectural, programmatic, and relational—not an afterthought.
