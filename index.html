Observatory Archive / Public Systems Strategy

Yesenia Pérez designs public learning and engagement systems that help institutions translate complexity into trust, participation, and access at scale.

At the Space Telescope Science Institute, I work across flagship NASA astrophysics missions, national learning networks, and public engagement initiatives, helping scientific institutions become more navigable, participatory, and human-centered.

My work sits at the intersection of science, governance, learning, and public meaning-making. I design structures that help people move through complexity without requiring insider fluency, from community-based astronomy engagement and educator networks to recognition systems and higher education pathway research.

I am especially interested in the layer beneath public engagement:
how institutions decide what counts, who moves forward, and what makes participation trustworthy at scale.

Current work includes
Roman Space Telescope public engagement strategy, Webb interpretive infrastructure, national informal learning networks, audience cognition frameworks, and research on institutional recognition and governance.

Selected Work

I design systems that make institutions more usable, participatory, and publicly legible.

The through-line across my work is not a sector. It is a recurring problem:

How do people move through systems whose complexity exceeds ordinary visibility?

That question shapes how I approach science engagement, learning networks, public trust, institutional recognition, and narrative design.

ARCHIVAL INSERT / METHOD

Across projects, the work is less about producing isolated outputs than designing orientation systems: the structures, stories, and relationships that help people move through complexity without surrendering rigor.

Case 01 / Launch-era public systems

Roman Space Telescope Launch & Public Engagement Strategy

Designing public entry points into a scientific future before the mission fully arrives.
Read case details
Artifact types:webinar planning · audience framing · host-site activation · facilitator onboarding · cross-network coordination

The Problem

As Roman moves toward launch-era visibility, public audiences and facilitators need orientation before the mission is already culturally legible. The challenge is not simply to explain the telescope.

The Intervention

I shape engagement systems that translate technical ambition into public orientation.

  • launch engagement strategy
  • facilitator and ambassador onboarding
  • host-site activation infrastructure
  • audience-facing narrative framing
  • interpretive guidance for non-technical publics
  • cross-network communication coordination

The Stakes

The challenge is helping audiences understand why discovery matters before they feel fluent in the science.

My work reduces cognitive and institutional friction between expertise and participation, helping communities, facilitators, and public audiences enter the mission without flattening the complexity that gives it meaning.

What This Reveals

Strategic public engagement is not downstream from institutional mission. It shapes whether institutions become culturally legible.

Case 02 / Interpretive infrastructure

Webb Telescope Storytelling & Interpretive Infrastructure

Moving science storytelling from information delivery toward cognitive and emotional orientation.
Read case details
Artifact types:STORIMap review · POI sequencing · guided-tour pacing · science brief refinement · audience cognition notes

The Problem

Technical precision can unintentionally obstruct participation when audiences lack a path through the ideas, visuals, scale, and stakes of the science.

The Intervention

I support interpretive strategy and audience cognition across Webb storytelling initiatives, including STORIMap interactive experiences and public-facing science interpretation.

  • audience-centered interpretive flow
  • conceptual pacing
  • narrative architecture
  • visual cognition and orientation
  • guided tour sequencing
  • emotional entry points into complex scientific ideas

The Stakes

I often bridge scientific rigor, audience cognition, narrative structure, and experiential coherence.

My approach treats storytelling not as decoration for science, but as infrastructure for understanding.

What This Reveals

Narrative shapes navigability. The stories institutions tell help determine whether people can imagine themselves inside systems larger than themselves.

DIAGRAMMATIC NOTE / SCALE

Scale is not only a measure of reach. It is a test of whether relationships, interpretation, and trust can survive beyond the first moment of contact.

Case 03 / Distributed public learning

NASA’s Universe of Learning & National Informal Learning Ecosystems

Building durable public learning infrastructure across distributed national networks.
Read case details
Artifact types:professional learning · partner communication · adoption pathways · evaluation-informed iteration · audience intelligence frameworks

The Problem

Outreach cannot remain one-way dissemination when the work spans museums, libraries, science centers, educators, and community organizations nationwide.

The Intervention

I steward strategy, partnership coordination, professional learning, and engagement infrastructure across NASA’s Universe of Learning informal learning network.

Instead of treating outreach as one-way dissemination, this work centers:

  • distributed participation
  • local adaptation
  • relationship continuity
  • educator-ready implementation
  • ecosystem cohesion
  • long-term engagement capacity

I design structures that help communities engage science in locally meaningful ways while remaining connected to national scientific initiatives.

  • webinar and workshop strategy
  • partner communication systems
  • engagement onboarding
  • adoption pathways
  • evaluation-informed iteration
  • audience intelligence frameworks

The Stakes

The goal is not simply reach.

The goal is durable public learning infrastructure that communities can use long after a single event or campaign ends.

What This Reveals

Public engagement scales when institutions distribute participation rather than centralize authority.

GOVERNANCE NOTE / PARTICIPATION

Participation scales when institutions distribute capacity, not just content.

Case 04 / Culturally rooted systems

Physics of Pō & Culturally Rooted STEM Learning

Treating culture, story, and scientific inquiry as part of one learning system.
Read case details
Artifact types:interpretive framing · narrative integration · relational learning design · culturally sustaining STEM engagement · community-centered inquiry

The Problem

Culture is too often treated as an add-on to scientific learning rather than part of the structure through which discovery becomes meaningful.

The Intervention

Physics of Pō explores what becomes possible when astrophysics and Indigenous Hawaiian knowledge systems are brought into relationship with care, rigor, and humility.

My work in this space focuses on:

  • interpretive framing
  • narrative integration
  • relational learning design
  • culturally sustaining STEM engagement
  • public meaning-making
  • community-centered approaches to scientific participation

The Stakes

It asks:
What forms of discovery become possible when institutions recognize multiple ways of knowing as structurally meaningful rather than symbolically included?

What This Reveals

Inclusion is not primarily representational. It is architectural.

Research & Ideas / Theory Wing

I study the governance layer beneath public learning.

My research examines how institutions operationalize legitimacy: what counts, whose participation becomes durable, and whether people can move forward without exceptional insider fluency.

Open Research Archive

Current research focuses on:

  • institutional recognition systems
  • apprenticeship and workforce pathways
  • transfer and articulation governance
  • public learning infrastructure
  • legitimacy and participation
  • public trust and navigability
  • institutional usability at scale

A central thread across my work is the difference between symbolic inclusion and operational inclusion.

Many systems claim openness while preserving ambiguity around obligation.

I am interested in what happens when institutions move beyond recognition as rhetoric and build structures people can actually trust.

Approach / Operating Principles

I work at the intersection of rigor, interpretation, and institutional trust.

My approach is grounded in a simple belief:

People cannot meaningfully participate in systems they cannot navigate.

Across science communication, higher education, and public engagement, I focus on reducing the invisible burdens institutions place on participation.

conceptual overloadhidden rulespathway frictioninterpretive gapsonboarding failuressymbolic participation without operational supportaccessibility as infrastructure rather than accommodation

I care about systems that preserve dignity under scale, not systems that survive through exceptional insider fluency, heroic labor, or institutional luck.

01

Clarity before velocity

Fast work can preserve unstable systems. Sustainable work requires clarity, alignment, and durable trust.

02

Story as governance

Narrative is not separate from strategy. Story shapes whether people understand systems, trust them, and can imagine themselves inside them.

03

Relationships as infrastructure

Large-scale initiatives hold through trust, coordination, and continuity, not technical execution alone.

04

Participation without dilution

I widen access without flattening rigor, expertise, or complexity.

Writing & Public Voice

The Echo Jar

The Echo Jar is a reflective writing and public-thinking project about science, governance, belonging, institutions, memory, and the emotional life of systems.

It is the reflective layer of the same questions that shape my professional work:

  • How do people remain human inside large systems?
  • What makes institutions trustworthy?
  • What survives scale?
  • How does wonder coexist with governance?
  • What forms of belonging become possible when systems are designed for dignity?

The writing moves between science, higher education, institutional ethics, public imagination, cosmology, leadership, and reflective inquiry.

The Echo Jar is not separate from my professional work. It is where I keep the human questions visible.
