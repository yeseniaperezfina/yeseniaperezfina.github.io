Selected work

The shape of the work I lead

The thread across my work is consistent: building learning systems, public experiences, and partnership structures that allow complex ideas to move well across contexts.

NASA’s Universe of Learning · National network leadership

Designing the structures behind a national learning network

I direct a distributed ecosystem of museums, libraries, and community institutions, shaping the supports that allow NASA science to be adapted locally without losing coherence at scale.

  • Partner enablement models, professional learning, and evaluation-informed improvement
  • Micro-grants, adaptation supports, and systems for sustainable local implementation
  • Translation of network feedback, reach, and impact into future strategy
James Webb Space Telescope · Mission milestone engagement

Turning high-visibility science moments into usable public learning

I helped shape community-facing strategy around major Webb milestones so launch-era excitement could become something educators and partners could actually use in public programs and community settings.

  • Resource kits, real-time learning assets, SME connections, and partner supports
  • Alignment across scientists, communicators, educators, and host sites
  • Focus on usability, timing, and local implementation — not just exposure
Cross-mission strategy · Translation and synthesis

Making complex science and evaluation legible to decision-makers

I translate dense scientific, operational, and evaluative material into briefs, decks, learning products, and framing documents that help teams decide clearly and move coherently.

  • Leadership-facing briefs and synthesis documents
  • Audience-centered resource and program design
  • Cross-functional alignment without flattening complexity
Museums and public learning · Early foundation

Building public trust through bilingual and intergenerational learning

My early museum work taught me the human-scale foundations I still use now: access, pacing, translation, context, and what it takes to make specialized content feel welcoming rather than distant.

  • Bilingual family programming and intergenerational design
  • Community-centered interpretation and partnership building
  • Institutional learning shaped by lived audience reality

Leadership

How I lead complex work

I lead with a systems lens and an editorial instinct: I want the architecture to be sound, the language to be clear, and the experience of the work to be usable for the people inside it.

What I protect

  • Clarity about purpose, roles, and constraints
  • Design decisions grounded in actual audience conditions
  • Structures that support both rigor and local adaptation
  • Enough reflection to improve, not just enough speed to deliver

What teams rely on me for

  • Calm judgment under complexity
  • Strong synthesis and language
  • Cross-functional trust and alignment
  • Seeing the pattern underneath the problem

Capabilities

  • Learning design strategy
  • Education and community engagement
  • Strategic partnerships
  • Evaluation and continuous improvement
  • Science translation
  • Executive writing and synthesis
