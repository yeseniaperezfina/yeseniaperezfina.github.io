Institutional strategy · Public legitimacy · Navigable complexity

Yesenia Pérez designs public learning and engagement systems that help institutions translate complexity into trust, participation, and meaningful access at scale.

At the Space Telescope Science Institute, I work across flagship NASA astrophysics missions, national learning networks, and public engagement initiatives to help scientific institutions become more navigable, participatory, and human-centered.

My work sits at the intersection of science, governance, learning, and public meaning-making. I design systems that help people move through complexity without requiring insider fluency: from community-based astronomy engagement and educator ecosystems to institutional recognition systems and higher education pathway research.

I am especially interested in the layer beneath public engagement itself:
how institutions decide what counts, who moves forward, and what makes participation trustworthy at scale.

Current work includes:
Roman Space Telescope public engagement strategy, Webb storytelling infrastructure, national informal learning ecosystems, audience cognition and interpretation frameworks, and research on institutional recognition and governance.

Selected Work

I design systems that help institutions become more usable, participatory, and publicly legible.

The through-line across my work is not a specific sector. It is a recurring problem:

How do people meaningfully move through systems whose complexity exceeds ordinary visibility?

That question shapes how I approach science engagement, learning ecosystems, public trust, institutional recognition, and narrative design.

Roman Space Telescope Launch & Public Engagement Strategy

Designing public entry points into a scientific future before the mission fully arrives.

As Roman moves toward launch-era visibility, I help shape engagement systems that translate technical ambition into meaningful public orientation.

This work includes:

  • launch engagement strategy
  • facilitator and ambassador onboarding
  • host-site activation infrastructure
  • audience-facing narrative framing
  • interpretive guidance for non-technical publics
  • cross-network communication coordination

The challenge is not simply explaining the telescope.

The challenge is helping audiences understand why discovery matters before they already feel fluent in the science.

My work focuses on reducing cognitive and institutional friction between expertise and participation:
helping communities, facilitators, and public audiences enter the mission without flattening the complexity that makes it meaningful.

What this work demonstrates:
Strategic public engagement is not downstream from institutional mission. It shapes whether institutions become culturally legible at all.

Webb Telescope Storytelling & Interpretive Infrastructure

Moving science storytelling from information delivery toward cognitive and emotional navigation.

I support interpretive strategy and audience cognition work across Webb storytelling initiatives, including STORIMap interactive experiences and public-facing science interpretation.

Recent work has focused on:

  • audience-centered interpretive flow
  • conceptual pacing
  • narrative architecture
  • visual cognition and orientation
  • guided tour sequencing
  • emotional entry points into difficult scientific ideas

Much of this work involves helping teams recognize where technical precision unintentionally obstructs participation.

I often operate as a bridge between:

  • scientific rigor,
  • audience cognition,
  • narrative structure,
  • and experiential coherence.

My approach treats storytelling not as decoration for science, but as infrastructure for public understanding.

What this work demonstrates:
Narrative shapes navigability. The stories institutions tell determine whether people can imagine themselves inside systems larger than themselves.

NASA’s Universe of Learning & National Informal Learning Ecosystems

Building durable public learning infrastructure across distributed national networks.

I help steward strategy, partnership coordination, professional learning, and engagement infrastructure across NASA’s Universe of Learning informal learning ecosystem.

The work spans museums, libraries, science centers, educators, and community organizations across the country.

Rather than treating outreach as one-way dissemination, this work focuses on:

  • distributed participation
  • local adaptation
  • relationship continuity
  • educator usability
  • ecosystem cohesion
  • long-term engagement capacity

I help design structures that allow communities to engage science in ways that are locally meaningful while still connected to national scientific initiatives.

This includes:

  • webinar and workshop strategy
  • partner communication systems
  • engagement onboarding
  • adoption pathways
  • evaluation-informed iteration
  • audience intelligence frameworks

The goal is not simply reach.

The goal is durable public learning infrastructure that communities can continue using long after a single event or campaign ends.

What this work demonstrates:
Public engagement scales most effectively when institutions distribute participation rather than centralize authority.

Physics of Pō & Culturally Rooted STEM Learning

Treating culture, story, and scientific inquiry as part of the same learning system.

Physics of Pō explores what becomes possible when astrophysics and Indigenous Hawaiian knowledge systems are brought into relationship with care, rigor, and humility.

My work in this space focuses on:

  • interpretive framing
  • narrative integration
  • relational learning design
  • culturally sustaining STEM engagement
  • audience meaning-making
  • community-centered approaches to scientific participation

This work resists the idea that culture is an “add-on” to scientific learning.

Instead, it asks:
What forms of discovery become possible when institutions recognize multiple ways of knowing as structurally meaningful rather than symbolically included?

What this work demonstrates:
Inclusion is not primarily representational. It is architectural.

Research & Ideas

I study the governance layer beneath public learning.

My research examines how institutions operationalize legitimacy:
how systems decide what counts, whose participation becomes durable, and whether people can reliably move forward without requiring exceptional insider fluency.

Current work focuses on:

  • institutional recognition systems
  • apprenticeship and workforce pathways
  • transfer and articulation governance
  • public learning infrastructure
  • legitimacy and participation
  • audience trust and navigability
  • institutional usability at scale

A central thread across my work is the distinction between symbolic inclusion and operational inclusion.

Many systems claim openness while preserving ambiguity around obligation.

I am interested in what happens when institutions move beyond recognition as rhetoric and toward structures people can actually trust.

Approach

I work at the intersection of rigor, interpretation, and institutional trust.

My leadership approach is grounded in a simple belief:

People cannot meaningfully participate in systems they cannot navigate.

Across science communication, higher education, and public engagement work, I focus on reducing the invisible burdens institutions often place on participation.

That means paying attention to:

  • conceptual overload
  • hidden rules
  • pathway friction
  • interpretive gaps
  • onboarding failures
  • symbolic participation without operational support
  • accessibility as infrastructure rather than accommodation

I care deeply about systems that preserve dignity under scale.

Not systems that survive only through exceptional insider fluency, heroic labor, or institutional luck.

Core Principles

Clarity before velocity · Story as governance · Relationships as infrastructure · Participation without dilution

Clarity before velocity

Fast work can preserve unstable systems. Sustainable work requires interpretive clarity, structural alignment, and durable trust.

Story as governance

Narrative is not separate from strategy. Story shapes whether people understand systems, trust them, and imagine themselves inside them.

Relationships as infrastructure

Large-scale initiatives hold through trust, coordination, and relational continuity, not just technical execution.

Participation without dilution

I care about widening meaningful access without flattening rigor, expertise, or complexity.
