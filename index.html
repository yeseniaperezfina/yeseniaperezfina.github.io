Selected work

What My Work Tends to Look Like

Across missions and institutions, my work sits at three intersections: national networks, launch and milestone moments, and the quiet, detailed craft of making science usable for educators and communities.

A few examples that show how I lead, translate, and support others at scale.

Leading a national informal learning network

NASA’s Universe of Learning · Informal Learning Network (~100 sites, 32 states & Puerto Rico)

Role: Strategic lead for network structure, partner support, and evaluation use.

Impact: Helped hundreds of local programs bring NASA astrophysics into their own community contexts.

  • Co-design participation models, micro-grants, and professional learning that scale across diverse sites.
  • Work with evaluators to turn adoption, reach, and impact data into decisions for future cycles.

Shaping community engagement around Webb milestones

Webb launch, First Images, and anniversary activations

Role: Co-led community-facing strategy, timelines, and resource design.

Impact: Contributed to a national activation of 600+ events across all 50 states and Puerto Rico.

  • Co-led community-facing timelines, resource kits, and SME engagement that fed into national activation.
  • Refined slide decks, activities, and guides used by partners hosting their own Webb events.

Translating research and mission science into tools

Hubble · Webb · Roman · NASA partners

Role: Research translator and synthesis lead across missions and partners.

Impact: Turned dense science and evaluation inputs into briefs and resources used by leadership and educators.

  • Convert dense inputs into clear narratives, decision points, and program models for internal and external use.
  • Maintain alignment across Hubble/Webb/Roman channels and partner institutions.

Holding space for families and communities

Museum-based learning labs & community programs

Role: Program designer and facilitator for intergenerational, bilingual experiences.

Impact: Built programs that welcomed thousands of families annually into sustained arts and culture learning.

  • Design intergenerational, bilingual programs that feel welcoming rather than intimidating.
  • Partner with local organizations so content, language, and timing make sense in context.

How I Work

My leadership style is calm, direct, and systems-minded: I try to make complex work feel navigable without losing the humanity inside it.

I’m interested in how learning happens, how systems behave, and how people feel inside the work. Every project is a chance to align those three a little better.

I treat every initiative as a learning system—for audiences and for the team running it.

I focus on making that system easier to navigate when stakes and timelines are high.

  • Systems lens
  • Pattern spotting
  • Steady in ambiguity
  • Care for people & pace

What I try to protect

  • Clarity about purpose, roles, and constraints.
  • Time to think, not just time to deliver.
  • Spaces where questions and concerns can surface early.
  • Design choices that line up with our stated values.

How people tend to experience me

  • Calm, direct, and not easily rattled by complexity.
  • Good at naming the pattern no one has quite put words to yet.
  • A bridge between scientists, educators, evaluators, and leadership.
  • Kind, honest feedback with the work—and the humans—in mind.
