Selected work

What My Work Tends to Look Like

I don’t focus on one-off events. I build patterns—ways of working that can hold real people, real science, and real constraints.

A few slices of work that show how I think and where I’m most useful.

Designing and tending learning networks

NASA’s Universe of Learning · Informal Learning Network

I help keep a national network of museums, libraries, and community organizations in motion—with clearer roles, better communication, and resources that work in real settings.

  • Co-shape pilots and models for how partners join, contribute, and grow.
  • Bring evaluation and partner feedback into decisions, not just reports.
  • Translate mission goals into something a local educator can pick up on a busy Tuesday.

Turning launch moments into learning moments

Webb launch, First Images, and anniversaries

I’ve supported the community side of Webb’s big moments—training, resources, and coordination that helped hundreds of sites host their own versions of the celebration.

  • Review and shape slide decks, activities, and guides used in libraries, museums, and community spaces.
  • Support hosts as they adapt content for their audiences and constraints.
  • Carry what we learned from Webb into how we’re thinking about Roman.

Making science usable for educators

Across Hubble, Webb, Roman, and partner programs

I sit between scientists, writers, designers, and educators—making sure the final experience is accurate, honest, and teachable.

  • Ask the “how would I explain this to a room of teens?” questions early.
  • Flag jargon, gaps, and assumptions that would trip people up later.
  • Align content with how people actually learn in informal settings.

Holding space for families and communities

Museum-based learning labs & community programs

Before space telescopes, I built programs in art museums. That still shapes my instinct: make room for family stories, language, and local context.

  • Design intergenerational, bilingual programs that feel welcoming, not intimidating.
  • Partner with local organizations so people see themselves in the work.
  • Bring those lessons into how I think about NASA’s presence in community spaces now.

Practice

How I Work

Underneath the projects, I’m paying attention to how people learn, how teams move, and what a system is quietly teaching the people inside it.

This is the part that doesn’t always fit on a résumé, but matters most on a team.

I treat every initiative as a learning system—for audiences and for the team running it.

My job is to make that system more honest, more humane, and easier to navigate.

  • Systems lens
  • Pattern spotting
  • Steady in ambiguity
  • Care for people & pace

What I try to protect

  • Clarity about purpose and roles.
  • Time to think, not just time to deliver.
  • Spaces where questions and concerns can surface early.
  • Design choices that match what we say we value.

How people tend to experience me

  • Calm, direct, and not easily rattled by complexity.
  • Good at naming the pattern no one has quite put words to yet.
  • A bridge between scientists, educators, and leadership.
  • Kind, honest feedback with the work—and the humans—in mind.
