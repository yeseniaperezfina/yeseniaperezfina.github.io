How the work moves

Editorial profile

A career built in the meeting point between knowledge and invitation.

My work began in museums, where interpretation had to earn attention. It deepened through public-facing STEM learning, where rigor mattered but accessibility mattered just as much. Today, I work at national scale — building the structures that allow science to move across institutions and into real lives without losing its complexity, dignity, or wonder.

I do not simply communicate expertise. I design the conditions under which it can be encountered, understood, and remembered.

That has meant leading cross-mission learning and engagement strategy for NASA initiatives, directing national partner networks, shaping resources used across museums and community settings, and building translation pathways between scientists, educators, institutions, and public audiences.

The through-line is consistent: I am interested in how knowledge travels. What helps it hold. What makes it legible. What turns a moment of curiosity into a durable relationship with learning.

Professional trajectory

A path from interpretation to ecosystem leadership.

The titles changed. The scale changed. The underlying practice remained: make knowledge meaningful, make systems hold, and make public engagement more intelligent than performance alone.

2015 — 2017
Family Programs Coordinator
Allentown Art Museum of the Lehigh Valley
Built bilingual, community-facing museum programming serving multigenerational audiences. This was the beginning of my long investment in access, cultural interpretation, and public learning that respects the intelligence of families.
2018 — 2019
Assistant Curator of Education and Experience
Worcester Art Museum
Designed exhibit-based learning labs and participatory experiences that strengthened collaboration between curatorial and education teams. The work sharpened my editorial instinct: ideas land differently when structure, design, and audience are considered together.
2019 — Present
Senior Education Specialist | Director-Level Strategy Lead
Space Telescope Science Institute
Lead education and engagement strategy across Hubble, Webb, and Roman. Direct national partner ecosystems, shape mission-facing learning architecture, prepare executive briefs and strategic syntheses, and build evidence-informed models that move discovery into classrooms, communities, and public imagination.
2024 — 2026
Master of Education, Educational Leadership
Harvard Graduate School of Education
Deepening the theoretical and organizational frame beneath the work: leadership, systems thinking, evidence use, institutional strategy, and the ethics of how learning environments are designed.

Leadership philosophy

The principles underneath the visible work.

Good strategy is not only directional. It is interpretive. It understands people, context, timing, language, incentives, and the emotional reality of how work actually moves through institutions.

01

Reflection is a leadership technology.

Pause is not passivity. It is one of the ways better judgment enters the room.

02

Equity belongs in system design.

Not as language layered on after the fact, but as a structural condition of how work is built.

03

Narrative is strategic infrastructure.

Story shapes trust, coherence, memory, and the ability of people to locate themselves within a larger project.

04

Evidence should guide, not harden.

Data matters. Interpretation matters too. Strategy lives in the space where they meet.

North star

I design the systems through which communities encounter science — with dignity, cultural integrity, and a sense of shared wonder.
