Selected Case Studies

A few projects that illustrate how I blend research, strategy, and design thinking to shape learning ecosystems.

NASA Universe of Learning — National Learning Ecosystem Strategy

Strategic Lead · National network · 100+ institutions

Context: Build a coherent, research-driven STEM learning ecosystem across a diverse network of universities, MSIs, museums, libraries, and community organizations.

Approach:

  • Designed and led mixed-method research: interviews, workshops, surveys, observational studies, and environmental scans.
  • Facilitated multi-stakeholder sessions with faculty, institutional leaders, front-line educators, and community partners.
  • Mapped learner journeys and space-use patterns across physical, digital, and community environments.
  • Translated findings into program requirements, partnership models, and high-level spatial and experience implications.

Impact: Produced national frameworks used by NASA and partner institutions to guide program design, staffing, and engagement strategies, aligning local context with shared STEM learning goals.

Webb First Images — National Activation & Experience Design

Lead Strategist & Coordinator · 100+ activation sites

Context: The first images from the James Webb Space Telescope called for a coordinated national activation through public institutions.

Approach:

  • Coordinated timelines, evaluation, and communication across more than 100 institutions.
  • Developed adaptable program frameworks and experience outlines partners could implement in their own spaces.
  • Integrated feedback and evaluation data into real-time adjustments and post-event learning.

Impact: Enabled cohesive, locally resonant public experiences across the network and informed future large-scale science engagement efforts.

Museum-Based Learning Labs — Intergenerational & Community-Centered Learning

Allentown Art Museum · Worcester Art Museum

Context: Design participatory learning environments that connect families and communities to art, culture, and inquiry.

Approach:

  • Developed learning labs and programs that supported intergenerational exploration and bilingual engagement.
  • Conducted visitor studies and observational research to refine spatial configurations and program design.

Impact: Demonstrated how museums can function as flexible learning environments and informed my broader practice in learning environment strategy.
