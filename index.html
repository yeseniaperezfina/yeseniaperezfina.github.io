Highland notes

On scale, weather, and work

A recent trip to the Scottish Highlands reminded me that good strategy feels less like control and more like reading the weather: watching for shifts, respecting the terrain, and making choices that hold up in changing light.

I tend to think in landscape metaphors. Networks behave like hills and watersheds. Programs behave like paths people take or avoid. Evidence is not a verdict; it is weather moving across a valley, asking us to adjust our plans with humility.

In my practice, that means holding three things at once: the horizon of a mission, the constraints of institutions, and the very local experience of a learner or partner. The Highlands offer a useful reminder: you can respect the terrain and still choose a line that is humane.
