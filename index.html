Editorial profile

A career built in the charged space between knowledge and invitation.

I began in museums, where interpretation had to earn attention rather than assume it. I learned early that information is rarely enough on its own. People need structure. They need context. They need a reason to care that does not insult their intelligence. That lesson never left.

I do not simply communicate expertise. I design the conditions under which it can be encountered, understood, and remembered.

Today, that work lives at national scale. I lead cross-mission education and engagement strategy, direct partner ecosystems, shape resources and experiences used across museums and community settings, and build translation pathways between scientists, educators, institutions, and public audiences.

The through-line is stubbornly consistent. I care about how knowledge travels. What helps it hold. What makes it feel usable rather than ornamental. What turns a moment of awe into a longer relationship with learning.

Professional trajectory

From interpretation to systems leadership.

The settings changed. The scale changed. The task stayed recognizably mine: make knowledge meaningful, make structures hold, and resist the lazy idea that public engagement is only about simplification.

Built bilingual museum programming for multigenerational audiences and learned, in very practical terms, that access is not an accessory to good design. It is part of the design.
2018 — 2019
Assistant Curator of Education and Experience
Worcester Art Museum
Designed exhibit-based learning labs and participatory experiences that brought curatorial and education work into closer conversation. This is where my editorial sensibility sharpened: structure changes what people are able to perceive.

Leadership philosophy

The principles underneath the visible work.

Good strategy is interpretive before it is declarative. It reads people, context, timing, language, power, and the emotional weather of an institution — then decides what can actually move.

01

Reflection is a leadership technology.

Pause is not passivity. It is often the point at which better judgment finally becomes audible.

02

Equity belongs in system design.

Not as a decorative layer of language, but as a structural condition of how work is built, shared, and sustained.

03

Narrative is strategic infrastructure.

Story shapes coherence, trust, memory, and whether people can locate themselves inside a larger project.

04

Evidence should guide, not harden.

Data matters. Interpretation matters too. Strategy lives in the charged space where the two meet.
