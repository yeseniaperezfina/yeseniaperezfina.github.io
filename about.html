About Me

I work where science, institutions, culture, and public learning meet. My practice is about making complex systems more navigable without draining them of rigor, wonder, or humanity.

Field Note

This is the orientation volume: the person behind the systems, the strategist behind the archive.

01

Practice

What I do

I design public learning and engagement systems that help institutions translate complexity into trust, participation, and access at scale. At the Space Telescope Science Institute, I work across flagship NASA astrophysics missions, national learning networks, and public engagement initiatives.

Method

How I think

I am drawn to the layer beneath engagement: how institutions decide what counts, who moves forward, and what makes participation trustworthy. My work asks how people move through systems whose complexity exceeds ordinary visibility.

Values

What I care about

I care about learning environments that feel usable, rigorous, culturally grounded, and alive. I care about systems that preserve dignity under scale rather than depending on insider fluency, heroic labor, or luck.
