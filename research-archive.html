Research

Recognition makes learning visible. Obligation makes learning count. This room gathers the governance questions beneath pathways, public learning, and institutional trust.

When does learning count?

My current research examines how institutions decide what counts, whose learning carries forward, and what kinds of agreements make pathways dependable. The focus is not only whether learning can be validated. It is whether institutions are obligated to honor that learning under clear, durable conditions.

Binding Recognition Infrastructure

A central idea in the work is binding recognition infrastructure: a pre-committed institutional obligation to apply validated learning toward named requirements. In plain terms, the system should not ask people to prove the same learning again after it has already been recognized.

Recognition Chain

I use a recognition chain to diagnose where movement breaks down:

  • learning
  • evidence
  • validation
  • translation
  • recording
  • application
  • advancement

The chain helps reveal whether a pathway is simply visible or actually reliable.

Institutional Usability

Many systems appear open while quietly preserving insider dependence. I study the hidden burdens people face inside complex systems: ambiguity, fragmented pathways, discretionary decisions, interpretive overload, and procedural expectations that make movement harder than it needs to be.

Public Knowledge Systems

This research connects to my science engagement work because both fields ask a shared question: can people move through complex systems without paying hidden penalties for complexity?

Research Question Can institutions become trustworthy enough that people can move through them without exceptional insider fluency, heroic advocacy, or luck?
